# BreakTest Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

BreakTest is an open source project. We want project spaces to be useful,
respectful, and welcoming for people using, discussing, testing, and improving
BreakTest.

This code applies to BreakTest-managed spaces, including the BreakTest GitHub
repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, documentation, websites,
and any community channels that Breaking IT designates for the project.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent where reasonable, and ask for clarification before
  escalating disagreement.
- Keep criticism focused on ideas, code, documentation, behavior, and impact.
- Be patient with people who have different experience levels or backgrounds.
- Respect boundaries when someone asks for a discussion or behavior to stop.
- Disclose relevant conflicts of interest when they could affect a project
  decision.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Publishing or threatening to publish private information without consent.
- Sharing private project or security information in public channels.
- Encouraging any of the above behavior.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it
privately to the BreakTest maintainers. Do not open a public issue for conduct
reports.

Reports should include enough context for maintainers to understand what
happened, such as links, screenshots, dates, and the people or accounts
involved. Maintainers will handle reports as confidentially as practical while
investigating and responding.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action they consider appropriate for the situation,
including:

- Asking a participant to edit or remove content.
- Warning a participant.
- Temporarily or permanently limiting participation in BreakTest spaces.
- Closing, locking, or moderating issues, pull requests, or discussions.

Enforcement decisions are made to protect the project community and keep
collaboration productive.

## Attribution

This policy is BreakTest-specific. BreakTest is independent from The Apache
Software Foundation and does not use Apache Software Foundation reporting paths
for BreakTest community conduct issues.
